RT90 is RT90 2,5 gon V (RT 38 and "Riktes nät" should be more or less the same thing. On old maps it only says "Riktets nät" and in the instructions you can use less numbers to denote a less exact coordinate)
UTM here uses gridzone and WGS84. After the gridzone comes northing and last easting in meters. Older coordinates often use older ellipsoids than WGS84 and the coordinate can be a couple of hundred meters wrong. Sometimes the MGRS gridzone is not used and instead only S/N to denote if it's the southern or northern hemisphere. Note that gridzone S is in the northern hemisphere)
MGRS AA scheme (new, usually with WGS84 or something close enough so it doesn't matter)
MGRS AL scheme (old, often not WGS84 but here interpreted as it so it can often be a couple of hundreds meters wrong)
Nearest city uses the 500 population cities from geonames.org (all cities with a population > 500 or seats of adm div down to PPLA4) so all credits to the geonames.org people.
